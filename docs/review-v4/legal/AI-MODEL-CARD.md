### 1.2 Intended Purpose

The AI Companion provides stage-appropriate parenting content to BabyMon users based on their baby's developmental stage. It also includes (planned) an on-device LLM chat feature that answers parenting questions.

**Current features:**
- Stage-specific advice cards (422 pre-written cards)
- Routine templates (13 templates across stages)
- Milestone expectations (200 expectations)
- Daily brief summaries based on baby's stage

**Planned features:**
- On-device LLM chat (code-complete, not yet deployed)
- Dynamic routine personalization

### 1.3 Intended Users

- Parents and legal guardians tracking children aged 0-24 months
- Parents tracking pregnancy (conception to birth)
- Users planning to conceive (pre-conception stage)

**Excluded users**: Children under 18, healthcare professionals making clinical decisions.

## 3. Performance and Limitations

### 3.1 Known Limitations

| Limitation | Impact | Mitigation |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Content not medically reviewed | Advice may be incorrect or outdated | Clear disclaimers on all content |
| Stage mapping uses simplified age calculation | Content may not perfectly match individual development | Content labeled by age range, not precise age |
| On-device LLM not yet tested on hardware | Quality and safety unknown | Not deployed until verified |
| Content coverage incomplete (~84% of target) | Some stages may have thin or missing content | Default fallback content provided |
| No personalization beyond stage | Same content served to all users at same stage | Planned: user feedback and preference-based filtering |

### 3.2 Content Freshness

Content is static and stored in the database. It is updated only when the application is updated or when administrators manually add new content. There is no automatic content refresh cycle.

## 4. Risk Assessment

### 4.1 Potential Harms

| Harm | Likelihood | Severity | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Parent follows advice that contradicts pediatrician guidance | Medium | High | Medical AI Disclaimer gate; "consult your doctor" on all content |
| Parent delays seeking medical care based on AI content | Low | Very High | Emergency warning in disclaimer; safety classifier in chat |
| Content contains outdated medical information | Medium | Medium | Stage-specific content, version tracking recommended |
| Parent feels anxiety from milestone expectations | Medium | Low | All milestones labeled as typical ranges, not deadlines |
| On-device LLM generates harmful advice | Unknown (not deployed) | High | Safety classifier, disclaimer, on-device-only processing |

### 4.2 EU AI Act Classification

Under the proposed EU AI Act, the BabyMon AI Companion likely falls under:

- **Annex III, point 11**: AI systems intended to be used for triage or diagnosis of medical conditions, or to recommend treatments — **NOT APPLICABLE** (BabyMon explicitly disclaims medical use)
- **Art. 52 (Transparency)**: AI systems that interact with natural persons — **APPLICABLE** (users must be informed they are interacting with AI)
- **Art. 69 (General-purpose AI)**: If on-device LLM is deployed — **POTENTIALLY APPLICABLE**

**Classification recommendation**: Limited risk / transparency obligations. Not high-risk under Annex III due to clear medical disclaimers.
