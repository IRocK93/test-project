Privacy Policy BabyMon

Effective: July 3, 2026 · Version 1.0 · End-User License Agreement

Summary in plain language. BabyMon is a parent-operated baby-tracking app. Parents are the only people who create accounts and enter data; we do not collect personal data directly from children. The app stores the information you choose to log (feedings, sleep, milestones, photos, journal entries) so you can see your baby's patterns over time. We use a small on-device AI to answer your parenting questions — that AI never sends your child's data to our servers. We share data with carefully chosen vendors for billing (Stripe), cloud storage (Amazon S3, Neon database), email (SendGrid), push notifications (Firebase), and optional crash reporting (Sentry). You can export, modify, or permanently delete all of your data at any time.

2. Data We Collect

We collect the following categories of information. Each category is either provided by you, generated by your use of the Service, or collected automatically.

2.1 Account information (you provide)

  • Email address — used as your unique identifier and for verification messages.
  • Password — stored only as a bcrypt hash; never stored in plain text.
  • Display name — optional.
  • Optional OAuth identifiers — if you sign in with Google or Apple through Firebase Authentication, we receive a verified user ID token and your email address from the provider. We do not receive your social-media password.

2.2 Child profile information (you provide, about your child)

  • Baby's name and a free-text "special move" field.
  • Assigned gender label (boy / girl / unspecified, stored in a fixed legal set of values that we do not free-form-store).
  • Date of birth, due date, conception date, or "idea" (planned-pregnancy) date.
  • Optional personality trait tags chosen from a curated list.

2.3 Tracking records (you provide)

  • Feeding logs (breastmilk, formula, solid food — timestamps, amounts when applicable).
  • Sleep logs (nap and night sleep — start time, duration, notes).
  • Health records (vaccinations, doctor visits, sick-day logs, allergies, medications, growth measurements including height and weight).
  • Milestones (what your baby did for the first time — e.g., "rolled over", "first word").
  • Journal entries (free-text notes about your day).
  • Photos and videos you upload — see the EXIF callout below.
About uploaded photos. Photos you upload to BabyMon retain their embedded metadata (EXIF), which can include GPS coordinates, device serial number, and the original timestamps. This information stays attached to your file in cloud storage until you replace the photo. If this concerns you, strip EXIF on your device before uploading, or contact us about a future server-side sanitisation option.

2.4 Usage and device data (collected automatically)

  • Push-notification token — a Firebase Cloud Messaging device token, used only to deliver reminders and feed/sleep notifications you have explicitly enabled.
  • App-internal diagnostic data — anonymous crash reports, sent to Sentry only if the app is configured to do so (we keep this disabled by default; see § 6).
  • IP address — used for rate limiting and abuse prevention; retained for up to 30 days in standard server logs.
  • Locale preference — the language you select in the app, used to deliver localized content.

2.5 Data we do not collect

We do not collect biometric identifiers, government-issued IDs, precise geolocation, contacts, calendar, microphone audio (other than optional voice input to the AI), photo-library contents beyond what you explicitly upload, or device advertising identifiers.

3. How We Use Your Data

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  1. To provide the core Service. Display your baby's tracking history, generate timeline charts, award XP and badges, and trigger reminders you have configured.
  2. To power the AI Companion. When you ask a parenting question, your question text is processed entirely on your device by a downloaded language model. See § 7 for details.
  3. To localize content. Match your recorded dates and milestones to stage-appropriate advice cards in your language.
  4. To process subscription payments. If you subscribe to BabyMon Premium, we use Stripe to bill you. We never see or store your full card number.
  5. To deliver notifications you have enabled. Reminders for feedings, sleep windows, and developmental-check milestones.
  6. To surface vaccination-related guidance. We display age-appropriate vaccination schedule information sourced from public-health authorities and your pediatrician's recommendations; we do not schedule auto-reminders for upcoming doses. Logging completed vaccinations is supported; reminders for upcoming doses are not currently available.
  7. To prevent abuse and protect security. Detect automated signup bots, brute-force password attacks, and unauthorized scraping.
  8. To comply with legal obligations. Tax records for paid subscriptions (Stripe retains these), lawful government requests (see § 8).

5. Children's Privacy (COPPA / GDPR-K)

Important. BabyMon is designed for use by parents and legal guardians. Children do not register for BabyMon accounts themselves. We do not knowingly collect personal information directly from any child under 13.

Because the Service lets you record information about your child (the baby's growth, feeding, sleep, milestones, photos), that information constitutes "personal data about a minor" under most privacy laws. The relevant rules are:

  • COPPA (United States). You, as the parent, are the party providing verifiable consent. We rely on your account registration as proof of guardianship. If you are registering on behalf of someone else's child (for example, as a paid nanny or doula), you must have the parent's documented authorization.
  • GDPR-K (EU/UK). Article 8 permits processing of a minor's personal data on the basis of the holder of parental responsibility's consent.
  • Other jurisdictions. Many countries have analogous rules; where required by local law, we will collect additional consent before enabling certain features (co-parent sharing, AI Companion, push notifications).

If you believe a child has registered directly without guardian consent, contact us at privacy@babymon.app and we will delete the account within 30 days.

7. AI & On-Device Processing

BabyMon includes a feature called the AI Companion, which answers parenting questions based on the stage of your baby and the data you have logged. We designed this feature to operate entirely on your device:

  • The language model (by default, SmolLM2 360M Instruct, Apache 2.0 licensed; optional premium SmolLM3 3B) is downloaded once and stored locally.
  • Your questions, your baby's data, and the model's response never leave your phone for processing. No chat content is sent to our servers, Google's servers, or any third party.
  • Upload of the AI model itself initiates from your app and passes through our backend only as an authenticated HTTPS download proxy for Hugging Face. We log only the model version selected, never the question text.
  • There is no central chat-history retention. If you uninstall the app, all chat history is removed with it.
The AI outputs are not medical advice. The AI Companion is for general information about development, play, feeding, and parental wellbeing. It is not a substitute for advice from a licensed healthcare provider. Always consult your pediatrician for medical concerns.

8. International Data Transfers

BabyMon hosts its primary database and most sub-processors in the United States. If you are located in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, Switzerland, or another jurisdiction with cross-border data-transfer rules, your personal data will be transferred to and processed in the United States.

We rely on the following safeguards for any such transfer:

  • European Commission Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) — module 2 (controller-to-processor) for our US sub-processors, as supplemented by the European Data Protection Board's recommendations on supplementary measures.
  • UK International Data Transfer Addendum — for transfers from the United Kingdom.
  • Region selection — Neon.tech and AWS S3 both support region-locked databases; EU users can request EU-only storage. Email privacy@babymon.app to request this.

For US government access requests, we follow the principles of the EU-US Data Privacy Framework and will challenge requests that are over-broad or non-specific.

11. Security Measures

We protect your data with a layered set of technical and organizational measures:

  • In transit. All client-server communication is over HTTPS with TLS 1.2 or higher; HSTS is enabled; modern cipher suites only.
  • At rest. Cloud-provider-managed encryption at rest for the database (Neon.tech / PostgreSQL) and file storage (AWS S3). Field-level encryption is applied to sensitive texts such as journal entries using the App's configured crypto.key.
  • Authentication. Passwords are hashed with bcrypt. Access tokens are short-lived JWTs (15 minutes default); refresh tokens are stored as bcrypt hashes in a server-side table and rotated on every use.
  • Authorization. Every request is checked against the user's relationship to the requested BabyMon record. Cross-tenant access is denied by default.
  • Audit. Administrative actions are recorded in an AuditLog table with actor, action, target, and timestamp.
  • Backups. Automated daily backups of the primary database, retained for 30 days, encrypted.
  • Vulnerability management. Automated dependency scanning (Dependabot); pgBackRest-managed backups; quarterly security review.

No system is perfectly secure. If you discover a vulnerability, please disclose responsibly to security@babymon.app; we aim to acknowledge within 48 hours.
